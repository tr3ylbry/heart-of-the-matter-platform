# Design Direction

## Visual Theme
Earthy, warm, soulful, transformational, human, musical.

## Logo / Motif
Liz uses the butterfly because it is personally significant through her mother and connects directly to the idea of transformation. The butterfly should be treated as a core brand symbol.

## Avoid
Do not use the lime green from the original brief as the primary color direction. It appears to have been incidental.

## Suggested Palette
- Warm ivory: #F5E9D0
- Deep umber: #3A2A22
- Clay / terracotta: #B76545
- Muted rose: #C98273
- Ochre / soft gold: #C99A3A
- Sage: #7E8A68
- Deep teal: #2E6670
- Ink black: #171412

## Logo Treatment
The provided butterfly logo is bold and graphic with a cream background and strong primary colors. For the actual site, consider adapting the spirit of the butterfly while shifting the palette toward earthier tones.

## UI Direction
- Warm paper-like background.
- Organic section shapes or wing-inspired curves.
- Subtle butterfly wing pattern as background texture.
- Strong serif/sans pairing or expressive headline font paired with clean body text.
- Avoid overly childish design despite serving children; the brand should also appeal to adult learners and educators.
